The Student Research Scholar Club (SRSC) at California State University, Bakersfield provides undergraduate and graduate students with hands-on research experience, faculty mentorship, and opportunities to present at national conferences.

Our Mission

🔬

Research Experience

Participate in faculty-led empirical research from literature review to data collection, analysis, and conference presentation.

🎓

Mentorship

Receive small group mentoring from faculty advisors throughout the full research cycle, building skills for graduate school and academic careers.

🌐

Professional Development

Present at regional and national conferences, earn research certifications, and build professional networks with fellow scholars.

🤝

Community

Join a close, motivated group of student researchers who collaborate, support each other, and share a passion for discovery.

Research Projects

2025-2026 Academic Year

Graduate

Air Quality and Worker Health Outcomes

Investigating how environmental air quality in the San Joaquin Valley affects worker well-being and organizational outcomes using experience sampling methodology (ESM).

Team Philip Wagner (Lead), Chris Sebastian, John Anthony
Undergraduate

Leader Workaholism and School Withdrawal

Examining the relationship between leaders' workaholic behaviors and working students' school withdrawal tendencies, with implications for student well-being and retention.

Team Jesus Arellanes (Lead), Eric Perez
N = 116
Undergraduate

Infertility Treatment and Workplace Support

Exploring workplace challenges faced by employees undergoing infertility treatment and developing HR policy recommendations through qualitative research.

Team Tina Cisneros (Lead), Cyrina Perez
Undergraduate

Leader Workaholism and Reproductive Health

Exploring how leaders' workaholism impacts female employees' reproductive health outcomes and workplace support perceptions.

Team Michelle Sanchez Ojeda (Lead), Quin Ngo

Join the Club

Starting Fall 2026, SRSC will accept new members through an application process. We are looking for motivated students who are passionate about research and ready to commit to a full academic year of collaborative investigation.

What You'll Gain

  • Faculty-mentored research experience
  • Conference presentation opportunities
  • Academic credit (MGMT 4850, 1-3 units)
  • Professional network building

Requirements

  • Enrolled at CSUB (undergraduate or graduate)
  • Committed to weekly meetings
  • Willing to complete IRB certification
  • Ready for a full-year research commitment

Interested? Contact the faculty advisor for more information.
